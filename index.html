IoT and ML-based Water Flow Estimation using Pressure Sensor

M. Gandhi • A. Mathew • S. Chaudhari • R. Shaik • A. Vattem
International Institute of Information Technology Hyderabad
INDICON 2023

Abstract

This study presents an Internet of Things (IoT)-based system that utilises machine learning (ML) techniques to estimate water flow through pipes based on pressure. The system incorporates an ESP-32 microcontroller, a Danfoss MBS 3000 pressure sensor, and a flow meter deployed at three locations to collect data for three months. To model the relationship between pressure and flow rate, ML algorithms such as linear regression (LR), support vector regression (SVR), and convolutional neural network (CNN) were trained, analysed, and compared. By establishing a model to estimate the flow rate based on pressure, the need for a flow meter in the setup can be eliminated. The system's low-cost, easy-to-implement, and non-invasive nature makes it suitable for widespread adoption in residential areas, offering a promising solution for optimising water distribution and reducing water wastage.

Keywords: correlation, estimation, flow rate, IoT, ML, pressure

The Challenge

💧 Water Scarcity Crisis

Water scarcity is a significant global threat affecting billions of people. Effective monitoring and management systems are critical to address this growing challenge.

💰 Expensive Traditional Methods

Traditional flow meters are expensive, require invasive installation, and are often impractical for widespread deployment in residential and municipal settings.

📊 Need for Real-Time Monitoring

There is a critical need for accurate, real-time water monitoring systems that can provide actionable insights for optimizing distribution and reducing wastage.

🔧 IoT and ML Solutions

IoT and machine learning offer promising solutions for automated, non-invasive water monitoring that can be scaled across diverse infrastructure environments.

Our Solution

Hardware Setup

  • ESP-32 Microcontroller: Core processing unit for data collection and transmission
  • Danfoss MBS 3000 Pressure Sensor: 0-16 bar range, 4-20 mA output, industrial-grade accuracy
  • Shenitech Ultrasonic Flow Meter: Ground truth measurement for model validation
  • Three Deployment Nodes: PH0100, PH0302, PH0303 at IIIT-H campus
  • Cloud Platform: ThingSpeak for real-time data storage and visualization

Machine Learning Models

Three ML algorithms were evaluated to model the pressure-flow relationship:

Linear Regression (LR)

Baseline model establishing linear relationship between pressure and flow rate.

Support Vector Regression (SVR)

Non-linear modeling capable of capturing complex pressure-flow relationships.

Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

2 convolutional layers, Adam optimizer, 100 epochs. Deep learning approach for pattern recognition.

Validation: k-fold cross-validation for robust performance evaluation across all deployment locations.

Key Findings

✓ All Models Achieved >90% Accuracy

All three ML models (LR, SVR, CNN) demonstrated R² values above 0.94 across all locations, validating the pressure-based approach.

✓ CNN Models Provided Best Results

Convolutional Neural Networks achieved the highest accuracy with lowest RMSE values across most deployment nodes.

✓ Strong Correlation Validates Approach

High correlation coefficients (0.97-0.99) between pressure and flow demonstrate the feasibility of eliminating expensive flow meters.

✓ No Calibration Needed

Models performed consistently across different locations without site-specific calibration, enabling easy deployment.
